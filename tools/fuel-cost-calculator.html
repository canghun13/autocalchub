How to Calculate Fuel Cost

The formula is simple: divide your distance by your MPG to get gallons used, then multiply by the gas price per gallon.

Fuel Cost = (Distance ÷ MPG) × Gas Price

What is the average MPG for a car?

The average fuel economy for new passenger cars in the US is around 28–32 MPG. SUVs and trucks average 20–25 MPG, while hybrid vehicles can achieve 45–55 MPG.

How to reduce your fuel costs

Maintain proper tire inflation, avoid aggressive acceleration, reduce highway speed, and keep up with regular maintenance. Each 5 mph over 50 mph can reduce fuel efficiency by 7–14%.
