How Much Does It Cost to Charge an EV?

Charging an EV at home costs significantly less than fueling a gas car. The average US electricity rate is around $0.16/kWh, meaning a full 75 kWh charge costs about $12 at home versus $26+ at a public fast charger.

Home charging vs public charging

Home charging (Level 2) is the most cost-effective option at $0.12–0.20/kWh. Public DC fast chargers typically cost $0.30–0.50/kWh — still cheaper than gas for most drivers, but 2–3x more than home charging.

How does EV charging compare to gas?

At $0.16/kWh and 4 miles/kWh, you're paying about $0.04 per mile. A gas car getting 30 MPG at $3.50/gallon costs about $0.12 per mile — roughly 3x more expensive.
